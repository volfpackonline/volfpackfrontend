1 Executive decision

Build Video Enigma as a vertical creator monetization product, not a generic text-to-video playground.

The best merged direction is to keep Video Enigma's concrete long-form-to-shorts pipeline as the product wedge and adopt VolfPack's provider abstraction, key custody, async orchestration, self-host distribution, and open-core monetization thinking as infrastructure.

Final product thesis: creators and agencies do not primarily want "AI video generation." They want more publishable shorts, fewer editing hours, repeatable channel growth, and eventually more revenue. Video Enigma should sell that workflow outcome.

What gets merged

  • From Video Enigma: channels, projects, owned-source ingestion, download, crop, split, clean, transcribe, rewrite, TTS, word captions, assemble, manual editors, local-network deployment.
  • From VolfPack: provider-agnostic model registry, BYO-key plus managed-key modes, durable async jobs, metering, cost visibility, self-host story, future hosted/open-core tiers, and honest moat analysis.

What to avoid

Do not dilute the product into a broad "generate any video from text" tool. Text-to-video can be an optional insert/b-roll provider later, but the core paid workflow is repurposing existing owned content into monetizable shorts.

3 Vision & problem

The problem

Creators often have hours of valuable long-form content but fail to convert it into short-form inventory because the work is repetitive, slow, and editorially fragile. The manual loop is familiar: pick a source video, find good moments, crop landscape to vertical, cut filler, rewrite for shorts pacing, record or generate audio, time captions, assemble, review, export, and repeat.

Generic AI video tools do not solve this. They generate new clips, but they do not understand a creator's archive, channel style, approval needs, publishing cadence, or revenue goal.

The vision

Turn existing long-form content into a predictable short-form revenue pipeline. Video Enigma should make a small team feel like a full shorts editing operation.

Why now

  • STT, LLM rewriting, TTS, OCR, caption timing, and video editing libraries are mature enough to automate most of the pipeline.
  • Creators and agencies already understand the business value of short-form distribution.
  • Local GPU and self-host workflows can reduce costs for heavy video processing while hosted tiers can monetize convenience.

5 Monetization strategy

Final monetization goal: sell more publishable shorts per operator-hour, with clear ROI against manual editing cost and channel revenue upside.

Pricing architecture

TierTargetPackagingRevenue logic
Community self-hostDevelopers, solo creators, privacy-first usersFree core, BYO keys, local storage, limited supportAdoption funnel, trust, OSS credibility, future upgrade path
Creator hostedSmall creator teamsMonthly subscription, managed hosting, 2-5 channels, usage limitsCharge for convenience and saved editing time
AgencyMulti-client production teamsWorkspaces, roles, approvals, client exports, priority queueHigher ARPU because every client adds recurring volume
Enterprise / offlineStudios, education, regulated media teamsAnnual license, self-host support, SSO, audit logs, SLASupport and control, not AI credits, justify contract pricing
Managed creditsHosted users who do not want separate AI accountsOptional credits for STT, LLM, TTS, stock/video insertsMargin on convenience, capped by transparent metering

Recommended first commercial ladder

  1. Free self-host: single workspace, BYO keys, unlimited local processing, no managed storage.
  2. Creator Pro: hosted app, 5 channels, batch jobs, managed storage, review queue, export presets.
  3. Agency: multi-workspace, client branding, approval links, team roles, usage reporting.
  4. Enterprise self-host: paid support, SSO, audit logs, private registry images, deployment help.

Why this is monetizable

  • The buyer can compare price to editor hours saved, not only to model API cost.
  • The workflow owns the revenue path from source video to short export, not just one AI call.
  • Hosted tiers can accumulate anonymized approval and performance data when users opt in, improving segment scoring and templates.
  • Agency workflows create natural expansion: more clients, channels, reviewers, storage, and exports.

Do not make managed AI credits the primary business. Provider resale margin is fragile. The durable revenue is workflow, team operations, analytics, support, and outcome-specific automation.

6 Goals, scope & non-goals

v1 goals

  1. Process a YouTube/direct video URL into one or more vertical shorts with generated audio and captions.
  2. Support multiple channels, channel-level default configurations, and multiple projects per channel.
  3. Expose pipeline progress for download, crop, split, transcription, rewrite, TTS, captions, and assembly.
  4. Provide human correction tools for crop, split points, scripts, and word-level captions.
  5. Use provider abstractions for STT, LLM, TTS, OCR, and optional video/b-roll generation.
  6. Run locally on a production PC via Docker, with a clean path to hosted deployment.

v1 non-goals

  • Full professional NLE replacement.
  • Native mobile app.
  • Automatic upload/publishing before review controls are reliable.
  • General text-to-video studio as the main surface.
  • Complex account system for the original local two-person deployment.

9 Provider & AI architecture

VolfPack's strongest reusable idea is not the text-to-video surface; it is the provider abstraction. Video Enigma should generalize this across every AI step in the shorts pipeline.

Provider classes

ClassPurposeInitial providersKey mode
DownloadProviderFetch source videos and metadatayt-dlp, PyTubeFixNo key / user auth later
STTProviderTranscription with word timestampsWhisper local, future hosted STTLocal or BYO/managed key
LLMProviderSegment scoring, rewrite, metadata, factual checksOpenAI first, LiteLLM-compatible providers laterBYO key or managed key
TTSProviderVoice generationElevenLabs, Bark localBYO key, managed key, or local GPU
OCRProviderText detection and cleanup decisionsEasyOCRLocal
VideoProviderOptional generated inserts, intros, b-roll, backgroundsVolfPack-style adapters laterBYO/managed/local

Adapter contract

ProviderAdapter
  capabilities()      # model, languages, max duration, cost hint, local requirements
  validate(request)   # reject impossible or expensive work before queueing
  submit(request)     # start job or run local task
  poll(job_ref)       # progress and provider state
  cancel(job_ref)     # best-effort cancellation
  normalize(result)   # common response shape for the pipeline

Key handling modes

  1. Local self-host: secrets live in .env; keys never touch the browser.
  2. Hosted BYO-key: encrypted per workspace with envelope encryption; user pays provider directly.
  3. Managed key: Video Enigma pays providers, meters usage, and bills customer with margin.

Moat shift

The defensible layer is not "we call many models." It is knowing which segments, hooks, caption styles, voices, and pacing patterns turn a specific channel's archive into high-performing shorts.

10 Product surfaces

Landing dashboard

  • Appbar with workspace/channel selector and global job status.
  • Create Channel and Create Project actions.
  • Channel sections showing the four most recently updated projects.
  • System health summary for local services, queue, storage, and provider keys.

Channel page

  • Edit channel name, description, source channels, and defaults.
  • Configuration grouped by pipeline phase for fast scanning.
  • Project grid with status, last run, generated shorts, and review count.
  • Analytics placeholder in v1; real performance charts in P1/P2.

Project page

  • Breadcrumb: channel name, project name.
  • Original source URL with copy/open actions.
  • Phase cards for download, crop, editing, transcription, rewrite, TTS, captions, assembly, and final review.
  • Each phase shows progress, current job, errors, retry, inspect, and editor entry points.
  • Primary action: Process Video, continuing from the next incomplete phase.

Editor pages

  • Crop editor: video preview, timeline, crop rectangle, dimensions, aspect ratio, AI/podcast toggles.
  • Video editor: lightweight timeline, cuts, segment markers, crop overlay preview.
  • Script editor: segment text boxes, AI rewrite variants, copy, factual check, TTS check.
  • Caption editor: collapsible segments, word/timestamp rows, preview with selected caption style.

Best architecture choice

Keep the Python FastAPI service layer from Video Enigma because it matches FFmpeg, Whisper, EasyOCR, Bark, and video processing work. Use a Next.js-style BFF and hosted frontend when billing, auth, and managed deployment become important. The current React/Vite frontend can remain the prototype surface until the hosted product needs stronger routing, auth, and server-side integration.

14 Metrics & KPIs

North-star metric

Approved monetization-ready shorts per active channel per week. This captures the actual customer outcome better than jobs run or AI calls made.

Product metrics

  • Time from source URL to first approved short.
  • Percentage of generated segments approved without major manual edits.
  • Pipeline success rate by phase and provider.
  • Average operator minutes per approved short.
  • Caption/script correction rate.

Business metrics

  • Hosted conversion from self-host installs.
  • MRR, ARPA, seat expansion, agency workspace expansion.
  • Gross margin after storage, compute, and managed provider credits.
  • Churn by failed activation, low output quality, or high AI cost.

Creator monetization metrics

  • Views, watch time, retention, subscribers, and revenue estimate per exported short.
  • Winning hook styles by channel and topic.
  • Revenue or growth lift per source video repurposed.

15 Risks & mitigations

#RiskLikelihoodImpactMitigation
R1Scope creep into full NLE or generic AI video studioHighHighKeep v1 centered on source-to-short workflow and four correction editors
R2Generated shorts are not good enough to publishHighHighDesign human review as core, measure approval rate, improve prompts/templates
R3AI/provider costs make hosted margins weakMedHighBYO-key option, local processing, transparent metering, charge for workflow subscription
R4Copyright or unauthorized repurposing misuseMedHighRequire ownership confirmation, source-channel allowlists, AUP, takedown workflow
R5Local setup is too hard for creator usersMedHighHosted tier, Docker Compose, health checks, guided provider setup
R6Video processing fails on edge-case codecs/resolutionsHighMedNormalize sources early, retain logs, add fixture corpus and FFmpeg tests
R7Provider API churn breaks STT/LLM/TTS flowMedMedAdapter isolation, capability registry, contract tests, fallback providers
R8Data flywheel conflicts with privacy self-hostingHighMedUse opt-in telemetry and accept that hosted tier drives shared learning
R9Shorts revenue is unpredictable, weakening ROI storyMedHighSell labor savings first, revenue upside second; expose performance analytics honestly

Blunt assessment

A horizontal VolfPack-style text-to-video tool is hard to monetize because it competes with model labs and has a weak moat. Video Enigma is stronger because it owns a repeated business workflow. The danger is building too much infrastructure and not enough publishable output quality.
